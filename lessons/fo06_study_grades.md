The first hours with a textbook move your score a lot; later hours move it less. That is a first-order system whose
"clock" is not time at all but **cumulative study hours**: $dK/dh = k(K_{max} - K)$. Knowledge climbs toward a
ceiling with diminishing returns, and the grade you can earn is decided by where that ceiling sits — which is a
property of *how* you study, not a fixed limit. A model for reasoning about effort, not a prophecy about anyone.
