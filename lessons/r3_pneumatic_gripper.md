A valve opens and compressed air fills a gripper chamber; pressure rises toward the supply line and the fingers
clamp. The first fluid-power example in the series, and the moment the electrical analogy starts paying rent: the
chamber is a capacitance, the orifice a resistance, and grip timing is a $\tau$ problem you solve by sizing the
valve.
