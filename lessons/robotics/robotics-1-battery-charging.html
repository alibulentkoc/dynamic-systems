Mobile Robot Battery Charging

A warehouse robot docks at 20% charge and fills toward full. As the pack nears capacity the charging current tapers off, so the state-of-charge climbs fast, then crawls — a first-order approach to a ceiling, just like a capacitor charging.

① The setup② The state③ The equation④ The response⑤ Takeaways

1 The setup

A robotics system that rises toward a set level.

An autonomous mobile robot returns to its dock nearly empty and begins charging. Early on the charger pushes hard and the state-of-charge (SOC) climbs quickly; as the battery fills, the current tapers and the last few percent take the longest.

2 Choosing the state variable

Track the stored quantity the rate law depends on.

The stored quantity is charge, read as state-of-charge SOC (percent of capacity). The charging current depends on how far SOC sits below full, so SOC(t) is the state.

3 Building the equation

The gain per step shrinks as the state nears its target — the signature of first order.

Full solution & interpretation

Shift to the gap g = 100 − SOC; it obeys pure decay dg/dt = −k g, so g(t) = g₀e−t/τ. Undo the shift:

SOC(t)=100+(SOC0−100)e−t/τ

Set the derivative to zero and the battery settles at 100%, where the current has dropped to nothing. The time constant τ = 1/k governs how quickly — after 3τ the pack is ~95% of the way there. Bigger battery or gentler charge current lengthens τ.

Takeaways

  • SOC is the state. Charge accumulates; the current depends on how full the pack already is.
  • Tapering current is the mechanism. dSOC/dt = k(100 − SOC) — the drive shrinks as SOC approaches 100.
  • Same shape as a charging capacitor. SOC(t) = 100 − (100 − SOC₀)e−t/τ, τ = 1/k.
  • The last percent is the slowest. After 1τ you are 63% of the way; ~95% by 3τ.
Quick check

Charging from 20% with τ = 20 min, roughly what SOC after 20 minutes?

One time constant covers 63% of the 80-point gap: 20 + 0.63×80 ≈ 70%. Practical fast-charge policies stop here and top off slowly, exactly because the tail is slow.
